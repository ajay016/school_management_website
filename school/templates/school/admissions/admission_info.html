Student Code of Conduct

This document outlines the expected standards of behaviour, responsibility, and mutual respect that every member of the Peace International School community is required to uphold. By enrolling at Peace International School, students and their families acknowledge and agree to these principles.

{# ── H2 ── #}

Core Values

Our community is guided by six foundational values that shape everything we do — from the classroom to the sports field:

{# Unordered list #}
  • Integrity — We act honestly and transparently in all our dealings.
  • Respect — We treat every individual with dignity, regardless of background or ability.
  • Responsibility — We own our actions and their consequences.
  • Excellence — We strive to do our best in everything we attempt.
  • Compassion — We care for others and act in the spirit of service.
  • Curiosity — We embrace learning as a lifelong journey of discovery.
{# ── H2 ── #}

General Expectations

All students are expected to arrive punctually, prepared with the necessary materials for each lesson. Disruption of the learning environment — whether in person or online — is not tolerated. Students must also:

{# Ordered list #}
  1. Attend all scheduled lessons unless formally excused by a parent or guardian.
  2. Complete all assigned homework and coursework by the stated deadline.
  3. Treat school property, equipment, and facilities with care and respect.
  4. Refrain from any form of bullying, harassment, or discriminatory language.
  5. Report concerns to a trusted adult — teacher, counsellor, or pastoral lead.
{# Blockquote #}
"Education is not merely the acquisition of knowledge; it is the formation of character. At Peace International School, we believe the two are inseparable."

— Dr. Amara Osei, Principal

Dress Code

Uniform Requirements

The full school uniform must be worn on all instructional days. It is a source of pride and identity for our community. Smart, clean, and complete — these are the three standards by which uniform is judged.

{# Inline image #}

On designated non-uniform days, clothing must still conform to the school's modesty guidelines. Branded sportswear is acceptable on PE days only when the student transitions directly from a sports session.

Consequences of Misconduct

Staged Response Framework

Peace International School operates a fair, transparent, and proportionate disciplinary process. The response to any incident is staged according to its severity:

Stage 1 — Verbal Warning

Minor infringements (e.g., minor uniform violations, lateness) are addressed through a verbal reminder from the class teacher. A note is logged in the student's pastoral record.

Stage 2 — Formal Warning

Repeated Stage 1 behaviours, or a moderate first offence, result in a written warning sent to the parent/guardian. A pastoral meeting may be arranged.

Stage 3 — Internal Suspension

Serious incidents or persistent disruptive behaviour lead to a period of internal isolation (1–3 days). Parents are notified and a reintegration plan is agreed upon.

Stage 4 — External Suspension or Exclusion

In extreme cases — including physical violence, possession of prohibited items, or gross misconduct — the Headteacher may issue a fixed-term or permanent exclusion following the school's formal exclusions procedure.
